Questions

Frequently asked

What is Dad Village?
Dad Village is a free, local-first web app that turns a curriculum of 84 science-cited lessons into a one-tap daily plan for dads raising kids ages 0 to 3. It schedules a mix of screen-free bonding activities and curated, developmentally vetted video content into four daily periods — morning, mid-morning, afternoon, and evening.
Is Dad Village free?
Yes. Dad Village is free to use, has no ads, and requires no account. All data — your child's schedule, streak, and journal — is stored locally in your browser, not on a server. Private by design.
Does Dad Village recommend screen time for babies?
For 0–12 months, no — the curriculum is screen-free by design, following AAP guidance that recommends no screen media under 18 months except live video chat. From 12 months on, Dad Village curates specific, developmentally vetted videos and schedules them alongside co-viewing guidance, rather than treating all screen time the same.
What makes Dad Village different from a general parenting app?
Every lesson is tagged to one of six executive-function "Compounding Pillars" (focus, impulse control, working memory, flexibility, self-regulation, delayed gratification) and cites the specific research behind it. Dad Village also uses a spaced-repetition engine that deliberately re-schedules key lessons and videos at expanding intervals, because repetition — not novelty — is how recall is built in young children.
