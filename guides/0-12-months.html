Screen-free by design: the first year

For the first year, the highest-leverage input isn't a video — it's you. Here's the cited, day-by-day version of what Dad Village schedules for babies 0–12 months, and why each piece matters.

TL;DR
  • The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends no screen media under 18–24 months except live video chat — so this age band is 100% screen-free by design.
  • Four daily anchors do almost all the work: skin-to-skin + narration (morning), tummy time (mid-morning), sensory/motor play (afternoon), and an identical bedtime sequence (evening).
  • Repetition is the point, not a limitation — the same four to five activities recur daily for weeks because frequency, not novelty, is what builds skin-to-skin bonding, neck strength, and a reliable sleep cue.
  • Every lesson has a no-fuss fallback for the days nothing goes to plan.

Why this age band skips screens entirely

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends avoiding screen media for children under 18 to 24 months, with the exception of live video chatting with family. Rather than treat that as a restriction to work around, Dad Village's 0–12 month curriculum is built entirely on what the research says actually matters most at this age: touch, voice, movement, and a predictable rhythm between dad and baby.

The four daily anchors

Skin-to-skin + narration

Morning · 15 min · Daily for 8 weeks

Shirt off, baby on chest, narrate what you see for ten minutes. This is the single highest-ROI activity in the whole age band — protect it.

  • Skin-to-skin ("kangaroo care") measurably stabilizes an infant's heart rate, breathing, and temperature — and lowers dad's stress hormones too.
  • Fathers who do skin-to-skin in the first months show stronger long-term involvement and bonding.
  • Your lower-pitched voice is novel and attention-grabbing to an infant — narration here is pure language input.
Cited from: Feldman, R. et al. (2014) on kangaroo care and parent-infant bonding; WHO newborn care guidance.

Tummy time + voice

Mid-morning · 8 min · Daily for 12 weeks

Lay baby on a firm surface, get down at eye level, and talk to them. Start with 1–2 minutes and build up — crying just means it's hard work, like a baby push-up.

  • Daily tummy time correlates with earlier rolling, sitting, and crawling — and reduces flat-head (plagiocephaly) risk.
  • Several short sessions beat one long one for building tolerance and strength.
  • Getting down at their eye level turns it into face-to-face social time, not just exercise.
Cited from: AAP "Back to Sleep, Tummy to Play" guidance; Pediatric Physical Therapy reviews on tummy time.

High-contrast visual time

Afternoon · 6 min · Alternating days

Hold a black-and-white patterned object 8–12 inches from their face and move it slowly. Newborn vision is blurry; bold patterns are the first thing they can truly focus on.

  • Newborns see best at 8–12 inches — about the distance to your face during feeding.
  • High-contrast patterns drive visual cortex development and teach visual tracking.
  • Your own face is the best high-contrast target there is — slow, exaggerated expressions work just as well.
Cited from: AAP infant vision development milestones; research on contrast sensitivity in neonates.

Bedtime wind-down + lullaby

Evening · 12 min · Daily for 12 weeks

Dim lights, quiet voice, one song, the same order every night. Keep it boring on purpose — bedtime is not playtime.

  • Consistent bedtime routines from infancy are linked to better sleep onset and longer sleep duration.
  • Predictability lowers cortisol — the routine itself becomes the sleep cue.
  • Dad doing bedtime builds a distinct bond and gives mom a real break.
Cited from: Mindell, J. et al. (2009) on bedtime routines and infant sleep, Sleep journal.

Frequently asked

Should babies under 12 months have any screen time?
The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends avoiding screen media for children under 18 to 24 months, with the exception of live video chatting with family. Dad Village's 0–12 month curriculum is built to be screen-free by design, using bonding, motor, and sensory activities instead.
What is the single highest-value activity with a newborn?
Skin-to-skin contact combined with narration. It measurably stabilizes an infant's heart rate, breathing, and temperature, lowers a parent's stress hormones, and builds fathers' long-term bonding and involvement — while your voice banks vocabulary for the language explosion to come.
How much tummy time does a baby need each day?
Start with 1–2 minutes several times a day and build up. Frequency matters more than length — several short sessions beat one long one for building neck, back, and core strength. Daily tummy time also correlates with earlier rolling, sitting, and crawling, and reduces flat-head risk.
Why does the same bedtime routine matter so much?
Consistent bedtime routines from infancy are linked to better sleep onset and longer sleep duration. Predictability lowers cortisol, and the routine itself becomes the cue that sleep is coming — which is why Dad Village repeats the exact same wind-down sequence every night rather than varying it.
